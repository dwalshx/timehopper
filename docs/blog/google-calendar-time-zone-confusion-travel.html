Google Calendar Time Zone Confusion: Why Meetings “Move” When You Travel

Google Calendar Time Zone Confusion: Why Meetings “Move” When You Travel

Direct answer: Meetings usually don’t “move.” What changes is the time zone you’re viewing the calendar in (display time zone) or the time zone the event is anchored to (event time zone). Travel and DST make these differences visible—and confusing.

The two time zones Google Calendar uses

Display time zone (how you see your calendar)

Direct answer: Display time zone controls what you see on the calendar grid.

Event time zone (what the invite means)

Direct answer: Event time zone controls what the meeting means for attendees.

How to prevent surprises

Direct answer: Use a stable primary time zone + a secondary time zone for travel/collaborators.

  1. Set Primary time zone in Google Calendar settings
  2. Enable Secondary time zone
  3. For location-anchored meetings, set the event time zone explicitly

FAQ

Should I switch my primary time zone when traveling?

Usually no—use a secondary time zone unless you’ll be away for a long time.

Why does one meeting shift but another doesn’t?

Because they were created with different event time zones.
