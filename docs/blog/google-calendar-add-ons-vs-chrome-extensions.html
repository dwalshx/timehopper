Google Calendar Add-ons vs Chrome Extensions: What’s the Difference?

Google Calendar Add-ons vs Chrome Extensions: What’s the Difference?

Direct answer: Google Calendar add-ons typically run inside Google’s ecosystem (often as Workspace add-ons), while Chrome extensions live in your browser and can give you toolbar quick-view, desktop alerts, and time zone tools more directly. Choose based on where you want the workflow to live.

What Google Calendar add-ons are good for

Direct answer: Add-ons are best when you want calendar features embedded into Google Workspace workflows.

Common strengths:

  • enterprise-friendly deployments
  • deeper integration with Google services
  • workflow automations inside Google UI

What Chrome extensions are good for

Direct answer: Extensions are best when you want instant access and fewer context switches.

Common strengths:

  • toolbar agenda / quick peek
  • meeting alerts on desktop
  • time zone conversion at a glance
  • faster daily workflow

Which should you choose?

Direct answer: If your pain is “I don’t want to open a Calendar tab,” you want a Chrome extension.

Use:

  • Add-on → for Workspace workflow integrations
  • Extension → for speed, quick view, alerts, time zones

FAQ

Can I use both?

Yes—many people use an add-on for workflow and an extension for daily quick-view.

Which is better for time zones?

Extensions are often better because they can keep “what time is it in X?” visible and accessible.
