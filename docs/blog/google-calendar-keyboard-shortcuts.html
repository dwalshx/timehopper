Google Calendar Keyboard Shortcuts: Full Cheat Sheet

Google Calendar has over 30 keyboard shortcuts for navigating dates, switching views, creating events, and searching. There’s one catch: they’re turned off by default. This guide covers every shortcut organized by category, with Mac and Windows differences called out inline.

How to Enable Keyboard Shortcuts

Keyboard shortcuts are disabled by default in Google Calendar. Pressing shortcut keys does nothing until you turn them on.

  1. Open Google Calendar in your browser.
  2. Click the gear icon (top right) and select Settings.
  3. Under General, find Keyboard shortcuts.
  4. Check Enable keyboard shortcuts.
  5. Click Save.

Once enabled, press ? at any time to open the built-in shortcut overlay. It’s a quick reminder if you forget a key.

Navigation Shortcuts

These shortcuts move you through dates without touching the mouse. They work in every view.

The j/k keys follow the same convention as Gmail and other Google apps. If you already use Gmail shortcuts, these will feel familiar.

If you show week numbers in Google Calendar, the g shortcut is especially useful for jumping to a specific week by date.

View Switching Shortcuts

Switch between day, week, month, year, schedule, and custom views with a single keystroke. Each view has two shortcut options: a number key or a letter key.

The number keys run 1 through 6 in order, which makes them easy to memorize. The letter keys are mnemonic (d for day, w for week).

If your week view displays weekends you don’t need, you can hide weekends in Google Calendar to keep the view clean.

Side Panel Shortcuts

The side panel (Google Keep, Tasks, Contacts, and add-ons) has its own keyboard shortcuts. These are the only shortcuts that differ significantly between platforms.

Jump to side panel (backward)Ctrl + Alt + ,Cmd + Option + ,

On Chromebook, Alt + Shift + . and Alt + Shift + , also work as alternatives.

If you use a time zone extension like TimeHopper, it adds time zone context directly in your calendar view without interrupting keyboard navigation.

Mac vs. Windows: Modifier Key Differences

Most Google Calendar shortcuts are single-character keys and work identically on Mac and Windows. Only shortcuts with modifier keys differ.

ActionWindows / ChromebookMac
Save eventCtrl + sCmd + s
UndoCtrl + zCmd + z
PrintCtrl + pCmd + p
Side panel (forward)Ctrl + Alt + .Cmd + Option + .
Side panel (backward)Ctrl + Alt + ,Cmd + Option + ,

The rule is simple: replace Ctrl with Cmd and Alt with Option on Mac. Every single-character shortcut (t, c, q, /, etc.) is identical across platforms.
