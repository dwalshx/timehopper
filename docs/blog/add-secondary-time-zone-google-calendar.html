How to Add a Secondary Time Zone in Google Calendar (So You Stop Doing Mental Math)

How to Add a Secondary Time Zone in Google Calendar (So You Stop Doing Mental Math)

Direct answer: A secondary time zone lets Google Calendar show two time zones at once in your calendar view, so you can schedule without constant conversion.

This is one of the highest-leverage calendar settings for remote teams.

Enable a secondary time zone (desktop)

Direct answer: Turn on Secondary time zone in Google Calendar’s settings.

  1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
  2. Open Settings.
  3. Go to General → Time zone (wording may vary).
  4. Enable Secondary time zone.
  5. Choose the second time zone (e.g., New York, London).

Which secondary time zone should you choose?

Direct answer: Pick the time zone you schedule with most often.

Common choices:

  • Your main collaborator region (ET if you’re in PT)
  • Your company HQ time zone
  • A key customer region

Avoid: Adding too many zones. Two is powerful; four becomes noise.

Best practice: label zones in your workflow

Direct answer: People make mistakes when time zones aren’t explicit.

Small habits that prevent mistakes:

  • Use “PT/ET” (or city labels) in meeting messages
  • During DST transition weeks, include two zones (“10am PT / 1pm ET”)

If you also travel

Direct answer: Keep your primary time zone stable (home base) and use secondary for travel—unless you’re away for a long time.

That way your weekly rhythm stays predictable, and you still have local context.

FAQ

Does a secondary time zone change my event times?

No—secondary time zone changes display, not the “meaning” of events.

Can I show more than two time zones in Google Calendar?

Google Calendar supports primary + secondary display, but you can use other tools (like a toolbar world clock) for more zones.
