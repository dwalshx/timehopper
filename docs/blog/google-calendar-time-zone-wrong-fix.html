Google Calendar Time Zone Wrong? Fix It Fast (5 Common Causes)

Google Calendar Time Zone Wrong? Fix It Fast (5 Common Causes)

Direct answer: If Google Calendar shows the wrong time zone, it’s usually caused by display settings, device time zone changes, or an event time zone mismatch. Fixing your primary/secondary time zone and verifying device settings solves most cases in minutes.

Quick fix (do these in order)

1) Confirm your display time zone (web)

Direct answer: Your Google Calendar view may be set to a different time zone than you expect.

  • Google Calendar → Settings → General → Time zone
  • Set Primary time zone
  • (Optional) set a Secondary time zone

2) Check device time zone (especially after travel)

Direct answer: Device “automatic time zone” can silently change how times display.

Confirm your device time zone settings on your computer and phone.

3) Check the event time zone

Direct answer: The event itself can be anchored to a different zone.

Open the event → Edit → verify time zone next to start/end times → Save.

4) Look for DST week effects

Direct answer: DST can change relative offsets between regions.

Double-check during DST transition weeks.

5) Verify you’re viewing the right calendar account

Direct answer: Multiple Google accounts can make it look like “Calendar is wrong.”

Confirm the active account across Calendar + extension + device.

Prevention checklist

Direct answer: The best fix is a one-time setup that makes time zones visible.

  • Set Primary + Secondary time zones
  • Keep a “what time is it in X?” shortcut
  • Add a reliable meeting alert
