How to Add Holidays to Google Calendar (and Get Them Back if They Disappeared)

Direct answer: In Google Calendar, holidays are usually shown via a holiday calendar subscription (for your country/region). If holidays “disappeared,” you typically need to re-enable the holiday calendar (or choose the right region) in Calendar settings.

This guide walks you through adding holidays on desktop and making sure they show up everywhere.

Add holidays in Google Calendar (desktop)

Direct answer: Enable the holiday calendar for your region in Google Calendar settings.

  1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
  2. Open Settings.
  3. Find the section for Add calendar / Browse calendars of interest (wording varies).
  4. Look for Holidays (sometimes under “Regional holidays”).
  5. Select your country/region holiday calendar.

Tip: If you travel a lot or manage multiple regions, you can enable more than one holiday calendar—but that can get noisy.

Get holidays back if they disappeared

Direct answer: Most “missing holidays” issues are caused by the holiday calendar being turned off, the wrong region selected, or viewing a different Google account.

Check these in order:

1) Confirm you’re in the right Google account

If you have multiple accounts, make sure the account you’re viewing is the one where holidays were enabled.

2) Make sure the holiday calendar is toggled on

In the left sidebar, look for a calendar named like:

  • “Holidays in United States”
  • “Holidays in [Country]” …and ensure it’s checked/visible.

3) Verify the correct region is selected

Some people accidentally enable a different country’s holiday calendar, or none at all.

4) Confirm you haven’t hidden “all-day events”

Some views or filters can hide all-day events, which is where holidays usually live.

Make holidays show on mobile too

Direct answer: Mobile usually reflects your Google Calendar account settings—but it may take a moment to sync.

  • Close and reopen the Calendar app after enabling holidays on desktop.
  • Confirm you’re signed into the same Google account on the phone.
  • If needed, force a sync in your device’s account sync settings.

Keep holidays useful (not noisy)

Direct answer: Holidays are helpful when they reduce surprises—not when they clutter your week.

A simple approach:

  • Enable one primary holiday calendar (your main country).
  • Add a second holiday calendar only if you routinely schedule with that region.
  • If your calendar gets cluttered, consider a “Holiday-only” view by toggling other calendars off temporarily.

FAQ

Why did holidays disappear from my Google Calendar?

Usually the holiday calendar was toggled off, a different account is selected, or your calendar list/settings changed.

Can I add holidays for multiple countries?

Yes—just be careful, because multiple holiday calendars can add a lot of all-day events.

Are holidays a separate calendar?

Typically, yes: holidays are shown via a subscribed holiday calendar you can toggle on/off.
