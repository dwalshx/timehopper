Google Calendar Dark Mode: How to Enable It on Every Platform

Google Calendar has a native dark mode (also called dark theme) on web, Android, and iOS. On the web, click the Settings gear > Appearance > Dark. That’s it. If you need platform-specific steps or the option isn’t showing up, keep reading.

How Do I Turn On Dark Mode in Google Calendar?

Open Google Calendar in your browser. Click the Settings gear in the top right. Select Appearance, choose Dark, and click Done. Dark mode applies immediately across your entire Calendar view.

Google added native dark mode to Calendar on the web in late 2024 as part of a Material Design 3 refresh. Before that, the only options were Chrome flags or third-party extensions. If you searched for this before and came up empty, the native setting is now available for all Google accounts — personal, Workspace, and Workspace Individual.

Which Platforms Support Google Calendar Dark Mode?

Here’s how dark mode works across all three platforms:

PlatformIn-App Toggle?PathFollows System Theme?
Web (desktop browser)YesSettings gear > Appearance > DarkYes, via “Device default”
AndroidYesMenu > Settings > General > Theme > DarkYes, via “Device default” (also follows Battery Saver on Android 10+)
iOS (iPhone/iPad)NoSystem Settings > Display & Brightness > DarkYes, automatically

The key difference: iOS has no in-app dark mode setting. It follows your system theme and nothing else.

How to Enable Dark Mode on the Web

  1. Open Google Calendar in your browser.
  2. Click the Settings gear in the top-right corner.
  3. Select Appearance from the dropdown.
  4. Choose Dark and click Done.

If you’d rather have Calendar follow your operating system’s theme, select Device default instead of Dark. When your OS switches to dark mode, Calendar will follow.

How to Enable Dark Mode on Android

  1. Open the Google Calendar app.
  2. Tap the hamburger menu (three lines, top left).
  3. Tap Settings > General > Theme.
  4. Select Dark.

On Android 10 and above, you have two extra options. Setting your phone’s system theme to dark will switch Calendar automatically if you leave the app on “Device default.” Enabling Battery Saver also forces dark mode system-wide, including Calendar and its widgets.

How to Enable Dark Mode on iOS

There is no in-app dark mode toggle in Google Calendar for iPhone or iPad. Dark mode comes from your system settings.

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Tap Display & Brightness.
  3. Select Dark.

Google Calendar will follow the system setting automatically. This requires iOS 13 or later. There is no way to set Calendar to dark mode while keeping the rest of your iPhone in light mode — it’s all or nothing on iOS.

What About Chrome’s Built-In Dark Mode Flag?

You probably don’t need it. Now that Google Calendar has a native Appearance toggle, the Chrome flag is a fallback, not the main method.

If you still want it — maybe the native toggle hasn’t appeared on your Workspace account yet — here’s how:

  1. Type chrome://flags in your Chrome address bar.
  2. Search for Auto Dark Mode for Web Contents.
  3. Set it to Enabled.
  4. Relaunch Chrome.

This forces dark mode on every website, not just Calendar. It’s an experimental flag, so Google could change or remove it in future Chrome updates. Once you have access to the native Appearance setting, switch to that instead.

If you use Chrome extensions to customize Calendar, be aware that the 2024 redesign can conflict with extensions that modify Calendar’s layout or styling. For help picking extensions that work well with the current Calendar, see our guide on what to look for in a Google Calendar extension or the best Google Calendar extensions for 2026.

Dark Mode Not Working? Quick Fixes

If dark mode isn’t behaving as expected, work through this checklist:

  • Appearance option not visible. Clear your browser cache and try again. If that doesn’t work, open Calendar in an incognito window. Workspace accounts may have received the Material Design 3 update on a delayed schedule. Check with your Workspace admin. As a temporary workaround, use the Chrome flag method above.

  • Chrome extension conflicts. Google warned that the 2024 redesign “may affect Chrome extensions that alter the visual look of Calendar.” If dark mode looks broken or partially applied, disable extensions that style Calendar — especially older dark mode extensions — and use the native toggle instead.

  • Calendar colors look different. Dark mode adjusts event colors for contrast against the dark background. Some custom colors may appear muted or shifted. This is expected behavior. You can adjust individual calendar colors in Settings > [calendar name] > color picker. For a deeper guide to color-coding, see how to color-code your Google Calendar events.

  • Dark mode reverts after a browser update. If you’re using the chrome://flags method, browser updates can reset experimental flags. Switch to the native Appearance setting to avoid this.

Does OS-Level Dark Mode Affect Google Calendar?

Yes. On all three platforms, Google Calendar can follow your operating system’s dark mode setting.

On the web and Android, select Device default in Calendar’s theme settings. Calendar will switch between light and dark whenever your OS does. On iOS, this is the only option — Calendar always follows the system theme.

This also extends to browser-based tools. TimeHopper, for example, respects your system dark mode setting in its popup.

Windows, macOS, Android, and iOS all support scheduled dark mode — dark at sunset, light at sunrise, or on a custom schedule. Calendar will follow that schedule automatically without any extra configuration. No need to toggle anything inside Calendar itself.
