London and Singapore form the backbone of Asian-European finance. Many global banks maintain their two largest international offices in these cities. Insurance underwriting, commodity trading, and wealth management teams synchronise across this 8-hour gap daily.

Singapore Time is UTC+8 with no DST. Greenwich Mean Time is UTC+0, shifting to BST (UTC+1) in summer. The standard gap is 8 hours: 9 AM in London is 5 PM in Singapore. During BST the gap narrows to 7 hours.

The overlap is tighter than it appears. A 7–10 AM GMT window maps to 3–6 PM SGT, covering the last three hours of the Singapore workday. During BST, the same London hours map to 2–5 PM SGT, which is even more comfortable.

Daylight Saving Time: What Changes

United Kingdom observes daylight saving time, but Singapore does not. This means the offset between GMT and SGT changes seasonally: 8 hours during standard time and 7 hours during daylight saving. Double-check the current offset during transition weeks in March and November.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the time difference between GMT and SGT?

SGT is 8 hours ahead of GMT. During British Summer Time (March–October), the gap shrinks to 7 hours. Singapore does not observe DST.

Does BST affect the GMT-to-SGT offset?

Yes. When the UK shifts to BST (UTC+1), the gap drops from 8 to 7 hours. Singapore stays on UTC+8 year-round. A 9 AM London meeting moves from 5 PM SGT in winter to 4 PM SGT in summer.

What is the best overlap window for UK and Singapore?

Aim for 7–10 AM GMT (3–6 PM SGT). This catches the afternoon in Singapore and the morning in London. During BST you can push to 8–11 AM London time for a similar SGT window.
