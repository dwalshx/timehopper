# TimeHopper Brand Voice Document (v1)

## Overview

TimeHopper’s voice reflects its role:

> A calm, precise, and trusted layer that helps people operate across time without friction or error.

We are not loud, clever, or hype-driven.  
We are clear, confident, and quietly powerful.

---

## 1. Core Voice Principles

### 1.1 Calm Authority
We speak with confidence, not volume.

- We don’t oversell
- We don’t exaggerate
- We assume the user is intelligent and capable

**Goal:**
Make users feel:
> “This tool has things under control.”

---

### 1.2 Precision Over Persuasion
We favor clarity and specificity over marketing language.

- Use exact words
- Avoid vague claims
- Say what the product actually does

**Goal:**
Reduce ambiguity and build trust.

---

### 1.3 Operational Empathy
We understand how users actually work.

- messy schedules
- coordination stress
- fear of mistakes
- cognitive overload

We reflect that understanding in how we speak.

**Goal:**
Make users feel:
> “This was built for how I actually work.”

---

### 1.4 Friction Reduction in Language
Our language should feel like our product:
- fast
- clear
- low effort

- Short sentences
- Simple structure
- Minimal cognitive load

---

## 2. Voice Attributes

| Attribute        | Direction |
|------------------|----------|
| Confidence       | High |
| Energy           | Low–Medium |
| Clarity          | Very High |
| Emotion          | Subtle (calm relief, not excitement) |
| Personality      | Understated |
| Complexity       | Simple surface, deep meaning |

---

## 3. What We Are NOT

### ❌ Not Hypey
Avoid:
- “revolutionary”
- “game-changing”
- “next-gen”
- “supercharge your productivity”

---

### ❌ Not Cute or Playful
Avoid:
- jokes
- puns
- emojis (in product or core messaging)
- overly casual tone

---

### ❌ Not Overly Technical
Avoid:
- jargon
- internal terminology
- overly complex explanations

---

## 4. Tone Spectrum

Think of TimeHopper as sitting between:

- **Apple system UI** (clarity, restraint)
- **Notion utility language** (functional, helpful)

Not:
- startup hype
- social media voice
- consumer app playfulness

---

## 5. Messaging Style

### 5.1 Default Structure

We often follow this pattern:

1. A clear outcome
2. A simple explanation
3. Optional supporting detail

---

### Example

**Good:**
> Never second-guess time again.  
> See the right time, everywhere you need it.

**Bad:**
> Unlock a powerful new way to manage your time across global workflows.

---

---

### Example Phrases

---

## 7. Writing Guidelines

### 7.1 Be Direct

**Do:**
> “Check time instantly.”

**Don’t:**
> “Easily and efficiently check the current time across different regions.”

---

### 7.2 Use Short Sentences

- Prefer 1–2 lines per idea
- Avoid long paragraphs

---

### 7.3 Avoid Over-Explaining

Trust the user.

**Do:**
> “Join meetings in one click.”

**Don’t:**
> “TimeHopper allows you to seamlessly join meetings with a single click, eliminating unnecessary steps…”

---

### 7.4 Use Active Language

**Do:**
> “Move events faster.”

**Don’t:**
> “Events can be moved more efficiently.”

---

## 8. Microcopy Guidelines (Product UI)

### Buttons
- “Join meeting”
- “Add event”
- “View calendar”
- “Check time”

---

### Tooltips
- Short
- Clear
- Functional

**Example:**
> “Shows this time in your local timezone.”

---

### Error States
- Calm
- Non-blaming
- Clear next step

**Example:**
> “We couldn’t load your calendar. Try refreshing.”

---

## 9. Emotional Tone

We aim to evoke:

### Primary Feeling
**Calm confidence**

### Secondary Feeling
**Relief**

Not:
- excitement
- urgency
- hype

---

## 10. Brand Voice by Persona (Light Adjustment)

### Operator
- More direct
- More precision
- Emphasize trust and control

**Example:**
> “Never get time wrong.”

---

### Planner
- Slightly more supportive
- Emphasize flow and structure

**Example:**
> “Make your day easier to manage.”

---

### Juggler
- Emphasize clarity and simplicity

**Example:**
> “See what matters right now.”

---

### Minimalist
- Ultra concise

**Example:**
> “Check time fast.”

---

## 11. Voice Tests

Before shipping copy, ask:

### Test 1: Is this clear in 1 second?
If not → simplify

### Test 2: Does this sound like hype?
If yes → remove adjectives

### Test 3: Does this reduce cognitive load?
If not → shorten

### Test 4: Does this build trust?
If not → make it more precise
