# How to Share Your Google Calendar (Without Oversharing)

**Direct answer:** The key to [sharing a Google Calendar](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/37082?hl=en) is choosing the **right permission level**. Most people don’t need “full details”—they just need to see availability.

This guide explains the permission options and a safe sharing approach.

---

## The three sharing levels that matter

**Direct answer:** Think of calendar sharing as a privacy dial.

### 1) Free/busy only (availability)
Best when someone just needs to schedule with you.

### 2) See titles and times (limited details)
Best for teammates who need context but not full notes.

### 3) Full details / manage calendar
Use only for trusted collaborators (assistants, co-owners).

---

## Share with a specific person (desktop)

**Direct answer:** Calendar sharing lives in your calendar settings.

1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
2. In the left sidebar, find the calendar you want to share.
3. Open that calendar’s **Settings and sharing** (wording varies).
4. Add the person’s email.
5. Choose the permission level.
6. Save.

**Tip:** Share the *right calendar*. If you have separate Work/Personal calendars, share only what’s needed.

---

## A “safe by default” sharing setup

**Direct answer:** Default to availability-only unless there’s a strong reason not to.

Recommended:
- Share free/busy for broad scheduling
- Share limited details for close teammates
- Avoid sharing full details unless necessary

If you want someone to book time with you, consider a dedicated “Scheduling” calendar instead of exposing your entire life.

---

## FAQ

### Will someone see my private event details?
Depends on the permission level you choose and whether events are marked private.

### Can I stop sharing later?
Yes—remove the person from calendar sharing settings at any time.
