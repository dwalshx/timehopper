# How to Color-Code Events in Google Calendar (So Your Week is Readable)

**Direct answer:** [Color-coding](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/11100067?hl=en) is one of the fastest ways to make Google Calendar **glanceable**. The goal isn’t pretty—it’s reducing decision fatigue when you look at your week.

This guide includes a simple system that works for busy calendars.

---

## The two ways to color in Google Calendar

**Direct answer:** You can color:
- **An entire calendar** (everything on that calendar shares a color)
- **Individual events** (overrides)

A simple approach:
- Use calendar colors for big buckets (Work, Personal, Family)
- Use event colors sparingly for “high-signal” categories (Deep work, Interviews, Travel)

---

## Color-coding system that scales

**Direct answer:** Fewer colors + consistent meaning beats rainbow chaos.

Try this:
- 3 base calendars:
  - Work
  - Personal
  - Family
- 2 event override colors:
  - High-stakes meetings (interviews, demos, doctor)
  - Focus blocks (deep work)

If you use more than ~6 meanings, it becomes hard to remember.

---

## Make your calendar readable at a glance

**Direct answer:** Glanceability is the real metric.

Quick checks:
- Can you spot your next meeting in under 3 seconds?
- Can you see where focus time exists?
- Can you see “high-stakes” blocks instantly?

If not, simplify categories.

## FAQ

### Should I color-code by meeting type or by person?
Meeting type usually scales better. Person-based coloring gets complex quickly.

### Do colors sync across devices?
They typically sync through your Google account, but specific behaviors may vary by platform/app.
