# How to Show Week Numbers in Google Calendar (ISO Weeks)

**Direct answer:** You can enable **week numbers** in [Google Calendar settings](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/6084644) so your calendar shows weeks as W01, W02, etc. This is especially useful for planning, project coordination, and teams that speak in “week of the year.”

---

## Enable week numbers (desktop)

**Direct answer:** Week numbers are a display setting you toggle in Calendar settings.

1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
2. Open **Settings**.
3. Look for a display/view section (often under **General** or **View options**).
4. Enable **Show week numbers** (wording may vary).

---

## When week numbers are worth using

**Direct answer:** Week numbers reduce ambiguity in planning.

They’re useful for:
- project timelines (“ship in week 12”)
- quarterly planning
- scheduling recurring work (“every week 2” patterns)

---

## If your team uses ISO week conventions
**Direct answer:** Teams should agree on the standard (ISO week numbering is common internationally).

If your team is global, clarify:
- Are we using ISO weeks?
- What counts as Week 1?

## FAQ

### Why don’t I see week numbers on mobile?
Some settings are view-dependent or app-dependent. If you don’t see week numbers on mobile, confirm settings on desktop and check the app’s display options.

### Do week numbers change my events?
No—week numbers are purely a display aid.
