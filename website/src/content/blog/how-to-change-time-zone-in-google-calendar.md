# How to Change Time Zone in Google Calendar (Step-by-Step)

**Direct answer:** To change time zones in Google Calendar, you usually need to adjust **(1) your display time zone** and sometimes **(2) the event’s time zone**. Most “wrong time” problems happen when those two are mismatched.

---

## Change your Google Calendar display time zone (web)

**Direct answer:** Your display time zone controls how times appear in your calendar view.

1. Open Google Calendar (web)
2. Click the **gear icon** → **Settings**
3. In the left sidebar, select **General**
4. Click **Time zone**
5. Choose your **Primary time zone**

---

## Show a secondary time zone (web)

**Direct answer:** A secondary time zone is the simplest way to stop doing mental math.

1. Settings → **General** → **Time zone**
2. Enable **Secondary time zone**
3. Choose your second time zone
4. (Optional) Rename it for clarity (“NY”, “London”, etc.)

---

## Change the time zone for a specific event

**Direct answer:** Event time zone controls what the invite “means,” especially for attendees in different locations.

1. Open the event
2. Click **Edit**
3. Find the **time** section
4. Change the **time zone** (often shown next to the start/end time)
5. Save

---

## FAQ

### Why did my meetings “move” when I traveled?
Usually your display time zone changed, while the event time zone remained anchored to a different zone.

### Why is Google Calendar one hour off?
That’s often DST or a display time zone mismatch.
