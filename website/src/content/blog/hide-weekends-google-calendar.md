# How to Hide Weekends in Google Calendar (Cleaner Week View)

**Direct answer:** If you mainly plan work time, [hiding weekends](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/6084644) makes your week view **denser and easier to scan**. Google Calendar offers view/display options that can remove weekends from the grid.

---

## Hide weekends (desktop)

**Direct answer:** This is a calendar view option in settings.

1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
2. Open **Settings**.
3. Look under **View options** / **General**.
4. Toggle **Show weekends** off (or choose a “work week” view if available).

If you don’t see a toggle, try switching views (Week vs Schedule) and re-check.

---

## When hiding weekends is a good idea

**Direct answer:** Hide weekends when it improves scan speed.

Great for:
- packed meeting calendars
- Monday–Friday planning
- “what’s next?” scanning

Not great for:
- family-heavy weekends
- retail/service schedules
- shift work across weekends

---

## If your work week isn’t Monday–Friday
Remote/global teams often have different “weekend” norms. If your team is distributed:
- make your week view match your actual working days
- use explicit time zone labels in messages

## Pair it with a faster schedule check
A clean calendar view helps, but the bigger win is reducing context switching.

## FAQ

### Will hiding weekends delete events?
No. It only changes what you see in the grid.

### Can I hide weekends on mobile?
It depends on the app’s view options. Desktop settings may not translate 1:1 to mobile views.
