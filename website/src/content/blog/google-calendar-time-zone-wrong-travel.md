# Google Calendar Times Changed After Travel? Here’s Why (and How to Fix It)

**Direct answer:** Your meetings usually didn’t “move.” What changed is typically your **display time zone** (how your calendar shows time) after travel. Sometimes the [event’s own time zone](https://support.google.com/calendar/answer/37064?hl=en) also causes confusion.

This guide explains the model and the fix.

---

## The two time zones Google Calendar uses

### 1) Display time zone (what you see)
**Direct answer:** This controls how times appear in your calendar view.

Travel can change your display time zone via device settings, browser settings, or your calendar settings.

### 2) Event time zone (what the meeting means)
**Direct answer:** Some events are anchored to a specific time zone (e.g., “9am London time”). When you’re elsewhere, you’ll see the local equivalent.

---

## Fix the “everything looks shifted” problem

**Direct answer:** First restore your display time zone to your home base.

1. Open Google Calendar on the web.
2. Go to **Settings** → **Time zone**.
3. Set your **Primary time zone** to your usual home/base.
4. (Optional) enable **Secondary time zone** for your travel destination or main collaborator region.

Then check one meeting that looks wrong:
- Open the event
- Verify its time zone (if shown next to the start/end time)

---

## A travel checklist that prevents missed meetings

**Direct answer:** Spend 2 minutes and you’ll avoid the “one-hour late” story.

- Confirm your primary time zone (home base)
- Enable secondary time zone for the destination
- Check 1–2 critical meetings for the correct event time zone
- Use a redundancy reminder (calendar + phone + desktop alert)

## FAQ

### Why did one meeting shift but another didn’t?
Because they may be anchored to different event time zones.

### Why is everything exactly one hour off?
Often DST or a display time zone mismatch.
